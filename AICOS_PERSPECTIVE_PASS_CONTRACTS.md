# AICOS Perspective Pass Contracts

## Purpose

Perspective passes are trigger-based counter-perspectives with explicit thinking contracts.
They are not free-form personas.
They exist to challenge a frame when the current reading looks too clean, too narrow, or too certain.

## Boundary

Perspective passes are:

- local
- shadow-only
- evaluation-first
- non-authoritative

Perspective passes are not:

- reviewer identities
- approval actors
- stylistic roleplay
- replacements for decomposition or review

## Contract structure

Every perspective pass must define:

- `pass_id`
- `trigger_conditions`
- `required_questions`
- `allowed_outputs`
- `forbidden_outputs`
- `failure_modes`

A mere persona name plus free notes is invalid.

## Initial pass set

### `constraint_skeptic`

Use when the current read contains many implicit assumptions, vague scope inflation, or unclear forbidden zones.

Required pressure:

- identify silently added restrictions
- separate plausible constraints from evidenced constraints
- surface unnecessary narrowing

### `counterframe_probe`

Use when the current problem feels too neat, the dominant interpretation arrives too early, or the team converges before testing rival frames.

Required pressure:

- propose at least one alternative frame
- identify what was excluded too early
- surface the most dangerous plausible counterframe

### `signal_gap_probe`

Use when the reasoning carries strong conclusions on thin signals or when polished synthesis hides missing evidence.

Required pressure:

- identify missing signals that matter
- distinguish absence of evidence from evidence of absence
- expose where certainty is overstated

## Forbidden outputs

A perspective pass may not emit:

- runtime commands
- registry actions
- approval language
- truth commitments
- persona-only prose without contract outputs

## Failure modes

Each pass must say how it can fail, such as:

- over-narrowing the frame
- inventing impossible constraints
- producing decorative skepticism without actionable findings

## Closure

Perspective passes exist to add disciplined counter-perspectives to the shadow track without turning counterthinking into roleplay theater or a second decision authority.
